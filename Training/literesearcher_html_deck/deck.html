LITERESEARCHER 03 / 30
PROBLEM · 04 / 30

Deep-research RL hits a cost wall.

Live search, browse, and crawling APIs make every rollout expensive — at scale, prohibitive.

FROM THE FIELD

“One round of on-policy RL with browse + search APIs can cost $59 K – $243 K in API fees alone — before counting compute, retries, or rate-limit overhead.”

B1
Throughput

Online search APIs take ~1.5 s per call and rate-limit at ~10 QPS. Browse tools like Jina take ~7.8 s per page. A single GRPO step needs tens of thousands of tool calls.

≈ 0.1×
throughput vs an offline mirror.
B2
Non-stationarity

The same query returns different pages on different days. Reward signals shift under the policy. RL convergence becomes unreliable; ablations are not reproducible.

drift
in both content and ranking, day-to-day.

Three families — each with a binding ceiling.

Online tools, local retrieval, and fully simulated webs all trade off cost, realism, and capability.

Live web APIs

+ STRENGTH

− LIMITATION

B · LOCAL RETRIEVAL

+ STRENGTH

Fast · cheap · reproducible.

− LIMITATION

Single-shot retrieval ≠ research; no follow-up reading or hyperlink traversal.

+ STRENGTH

Cheap and controlled · reproducible.

− LIMITATION

Tiny corpus · synthetic distribution · poor sim2real for open-ended research.

THESIS · 06 / 30

Mirror the web · isolate the noise.

Take the realism of the open web · take the throughput of a simulator · refuse the trade-off.

T1
Mirroring

Snapshot the open web (32 M pages · 1 M+ domains) into a local read-only corpus. The policy queries a faithful approximation of the real distribution — without ever leaving the cluster.

T2
Isolation

Replace flaky network calls with deterministic local services: search returns the same hits, browse returns the same body. Reward signals become reproducible — on-policy RL converges.

RESULTING OUTCOME (4 B model)
GAIA 71.3
Xbench-DS 78.0
Frames 83.1
Matches or beats commercial deep-research products at < 1 % of their cost.

METHOD · 07 / 30

Three pillars — composed as a single training stack.

Data, environment, and learning algorithm are co-designed; each pillar removes a bottleneck of the previous one.

P1
Atomic-capability data

Synthesize trajectories around five atomic capabilities — search, browse, multi-hop reasoning, evidence integration, refusal — at million-trajectory scale.

5 capabilities
composable into open-ended tasks.
P2
Local tool environment

32 M-page mirror, faiss + BM25 hybrid index, lightweight browse extractor. Tens of thousands of RL rollouts per step are now cheap.

73.2 M
tool calls across the full training run.
P3
Curriculum + on-policy GRPO

Stage 1: easier tasks, 32k context — stabilize tool use. Stage 2: harder tasks, 48k context — extend depth and self-correction.

2 stages
staged context & difficulty.
EMERGENT BEHAVIOR

LITERESEARCHER 07 / 30
PILLAR 1 · 08 / 30

A 4-step pipeline that scales data, not labelers.

Each step is mechanical and auditable — quality and volume are decoupled.

STEP 1
Seed

Sample query intents from Wikipedia article structure (titles, sections, infoboxes).

VOLUME
~ 1.2 M intents
STEP 2
Mask

Apply factoid masks to surface gold answers and unambiguous question forms.

PRINCIPLE
single-answer ★
STEP 3
Filter

Verify answer recoverability with a small LM rollout; discard tasks the env cannot answer.

KEPT
~ 38 % pass-through
STEP 4
Expand

Compose atomic tasks into multi-hop, multi-evidence problems; teacher writes a trajectory.

OUTPUT
~ 480 k trajectories
DESIGN RATIONALE
Volume is unlocked by mechanical steps; quality is enforced by the Filter stage — not by hand-labeling. The result is a pipeline that scales linearly with compute, not with annotator headcount.
LITERESEARCHER 08 / 30
PILLAR 1 · 09 / 30

Five atomic capabilities — every research task decomposes.

Each capability is supervised independently; composition is left to the policy.

C1
Targeted search

Translate intent into discriminative queries; iterate over keywords until evidence surfaces.

INVARIANT
no hallucinated URLs
C2
Deep browsing

Open promising hits, read structured content, follow hyperlinks for cross-checking.

INVARIANT
cite extracted spans
C3
Multi-hop reasoning

Chain partial evidence into a derivation; carry constraints across turns.

INVARIANT
explicit reasoning steps
C4
Evidence integration

Reconcile multiple sources; resolve contradictions and weight by reliability.

INVARIANT
verifiable provenance
C5
Calibrated refusal

Abstain when evidence is insufficient; report uncertainty instead of fabricating.

INVARIANT
honest "I don't know"
COMPOSITION
An open-ended GAIA task is just a learned sequence of these five primitives — supervised at the atom, generalized at the composition.

LITERESEARCHER 16 / 30
ABLATION 3 / 5 · 17 / 30

On-policy beats off-policy — only because the env is fast enough.

Without sub-second tool latency, the on-policy schedule would not finish in any reasonable time.

WHY ON-POLICY MATTERS HERE

When the rollout source matches the policy being trained, the advantage estimate is unbiased and the policy keeps moving toward higher-reward regions of the trajectory space.

Off-policy RL (reuse old rollouts) saves compute but injects a slow drift: as the policy improves, its old trajectories become less representative, and the gradient grows biased.

PRECONDITION

On-policy is only practical when tool latency is sub-second — a property of the local mirror, not of the algorithm.

LITERESEARCHER 28 / 30
TAKEAWAYS · 29 / 30

Three things to remember.

If the deck is forgotten, the following three claims still stand on their own.

01
Local environments are enough.

A 32 M-page mirror + a lightweight browse tool replaces $59 K–$243 K of commercial APIs, delivers 10×–46× speed-ups, and unlocks the trajectory volume needed for on-policy RL at scale.

02
Simple-but-scalable beats clever-but-fragile.

Atomic-capability synthesis, on-policy GRPO, and a 2-stage curriculum — each individually familiar — combine to lift a 4 B model past 30 B teachers and most commercial systems.

03
The gain has a behavioral signature.

Two structural mechanisms (tool-choice shift, trajectory re-allocation) act first; depth and self-correction emerge as their downstream effect. Sim2Real is sustained because the behavior — not just the score — transfers.
